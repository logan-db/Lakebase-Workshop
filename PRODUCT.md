# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Workshop attendees: Solutions Architects, data engineers, ML engineers, and platform architects learning Databricks Lakebase Autoscaling through hands-on labs. They arrive with strong technical backgrounds but may be seeing Lakebase for the first time. They're using this Lab Console app during a facilitated or self-paced workshop, typically on a laptop at a desk. The goal is to build real understanding by doing, not just reading.

## Product Purpose

The Lakebase Lab Console is an interactive companion to the Lakebase Autoscaling Workshop. It mirrors every lab notebook in a visual interface, letting attendees explore data operations, autoscaling behavior, branching, reverse ETL, agent memory, observability, and more without leaving the browser. Success means an attendee finishes the workshop feeling confident they understand Lakebase well enough to evaluate it for their own workloads.

## Brand Personality

Bold, Energetic, Modern. The interface should feel like a capable tool built by engineers who care about craft. It communicates competence through clarity and energy through purposeful design choices, not decorative excess. The tone is direct and confident without being cold.

## Anti-references

- Enterprise-heavy dashboards: dense, cluttered, gray corporate UIs with too many panels, tiny text, and walls of data. This should breathe.
- Generic SaaS dashboards: the Stripe/Linear clone aesthetic where every product UI converges on the same muted palette, identical card grids, and safe typographic choices. This should have its own identity.

## Design Principles

1. **Confidence through clarity**: Every screen should make the user feel oriented. Clear hierarchy, obvious actions, no guessing what's interactive.
2. **Show the system, not the UI**: The interface should get out of the way and let Lakebase's behavior (autoscaling, branching, syncing) be the star. Data and system state front and center.
3. **Energy without noise**: Bold doesn't mean loud. Use color, weight, and spacing to create energy. Avoid clutter, redundancy, and decoration that doesn't serve comprehension.
4. **Workshop-paced**: Design for someone working through exercises. Clear starting points, visible progress, and enough context to proceed without asking the facilitator.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Basic accessibility for workshop context. Ensure sufficient contrast for readability, logical tab order, and no interaction patterns that require a mouse. Full WCAG AA compliance is a nice-to-have but not a hard requirement for this internal workshop tool.
